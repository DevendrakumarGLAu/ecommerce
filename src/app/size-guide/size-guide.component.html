Perfect Fit Guide

Find Your Bangle Size

Traditional Firozabad bangles come in standard Indian sizing. Use our guide to measure your wrist and find your perfect fit.

Step-by-Step

How to Measure Your Wrist

Choose your preferred measuring method below

1

Wrap a string

Wrap a non-elastic string or thread snugly around the widest part of your hand — across your knuckles with your fingers held together.

2

Mark the overlap

Mark the point where the string meets with a pen. Keep the string just snug — not tight. Your hand should be able to curl slightly.

3

Measure the length

Lay the string flat against a ruler. The length in millimetres is your circumference. Use the chart below to find your size.

4

Find your size

Match your circumference measurement in the size chart. If between two sizes, choose the larger one for comfort.

1

Cut a paper strip

Cut a strip of paper about 1 cm wide and at least 20 cm long. A receipt or notebook margin works perfectly.

2

Wrap around knuckles

Wrap the paper strip around the widest part of your hand — across all four knuckles with fingers together and thumb tucked.

3

Mark and measure

Mark where the paper overlaps. Lay flat and measure from one end to your mark. Note the measurement in mm.

4

Match to chart

Find your circumference in the size chart below. Round up to the next size if your measurement falls between two sizes.

1

Find a well-fitting bangle

Pick a bangle you already own that fits comfortably — it should pass over your knuckles with slight resistance, not loose or painfully tight.

2

Measure the inner diameter

Using a ruler or measuring tape, measure the inside diameter of the bangle — from one inner edge straight across to the opposite inner edge.

3

Note the diameter in mm

Convert to millimetres if needed (1 cm = 10 mm). This diameter measurement maps directly to our size chart.

4

Find your size

Match the diameter in the chart below. Our bangles follow standard Indian sizing so your measurement will correspond exactly.
